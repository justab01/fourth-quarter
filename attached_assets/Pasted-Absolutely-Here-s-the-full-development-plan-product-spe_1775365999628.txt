3. Core product problems to solve

Problem 1: Structure is there, but depth is shallow

A lot of pages exist, but they stop one layer too early.

Example:
	•	football page exists
	•	player page exists
	•	game log exists
but the log is not premium, segmented, or mobile-optimized

Problem 2: Routing is unreliable

Some taps lead to dead screens, “not found,” or incomplete destinations.

Problem 3: Features are not systemized

Draft, game logs, player pages, sports hubs, and team views do not yet feel like parts of one unified architecture.

Problem 4: Mobile hierarchy is weak

Some views are too dense, too flat, or too banner-heavy.

Problem 5: Cross-sport logic is inconsistent

NFL draft has more thought than the rest, but the app must be equally intelligent for NBA, MLB, NHL, soccer, golf, tennis, combat, etc.

4. Product vision going forward

You need to rebuild the app around 5 master pillars:

Pillar A — Home / discovery

What matters right now

Pillar B — Sport / league operating system

Everything happening in a sport

Pillar C — Team intelligence

Roster, needs, standings, future, schedule

Pillar D — Player intelligence

Stats, logs, splits, news, history, achievements

Pillar E — Future pipeline

Drafts, prospects, assets, transfers, recruitment

That is the real product.

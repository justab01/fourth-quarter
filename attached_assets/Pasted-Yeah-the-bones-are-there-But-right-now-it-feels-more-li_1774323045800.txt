# What feels weak right now

## 1. It feels too boxed in

Almost everything is inside rounded dark cards with similar treatment.
So visually, page after page starts to blend together.

Result:

* nothing feels special
* nothing feels urgent
* nothing feels “hero”
* everything feels same priority

## 2. The home page doesn’t yet feel like *my* hub

Your Hub is supposed to be personalized around favorite teams and recommended news , but visually it still feels like a general sports app home, not a sports OS that knows me.

It should feel like:

* **my teams**
* **my live alerts**
* **my storylines**
* **my momentum**
* **my next games**
* **my watchlist**

Right now it feels more like “sports homepage with user name.”

## 3. The typography hierarchy is too flat

A lot of the text weights and sizes are close together.
That makes the product feel less premium and less quick to scan.

## 4. Your news cards feel standard

This is a huge one.

Your news system is supposed to be one of the signature differentiators because of the AI reading modes — Pro, Simple, Toddler, Translate . But visually, the news feed still feels like a normal article list.

That means the product is underselling one of its best ideas.

## 5. Standings page is functional, not elite

It works, but it doesn’t feel powerful.
It looks like a table dropped into mobile. Good enough isn’t enough there.

## 6. Profile page feels underdeveloped

This should be where the app becomes yours.
Instead it currently reads more like settings with a badge.

## 7. The app doesn’t yet have enough “motion identity”

A next-gen sports app should feel reactive:

* scores ticking
* cards breathing
* chips responding
* live states pulsing
* transitions feeling intentional

Not overanimated. Just alive.

# What I would change page by page

## 1. HUB / HOME — make this the killer page

This page needs to destroy ESPN and Sofascore emotionally.

### New structure:

**A. Dynamic Hero**
At the top, instead of one generic featured game card, make it a modular hero area:

* if favorite team is live: show that game
* if no favorite team live: show biggest game
* if no live games: show next game / biggest headline
* if breaking news: hero becomes breaking news strip

This hero should feel cinematic:

* larger score
* bigger logos
* smarter spacing
* contextual subtitle like:

  * “4Q • 6:04 left”
  * “Final • OT”
  * “Starts in 32m”
* optional mini momentum bar

### B. “For You Right Now”

Not “Today’s Games.”
That’s generic.

Make a section that changes based on context:

* Live now
* Starting soon
* News affecting your teams
* Playoff race changes
* Injury updates
* Bet-worthy slate

### C. Quick modules

Below hero:

* My Teams
* Watchlist
* League Pulse
* Top Storyline
* Tonight’s Slate
* Trending Players

Let users reorder these later.

### D. Replace generic article stack with story clusters

Instead of card after card after card:

* “Rockets”
* “NBA Playoff Race”
* “NFL Free Agency”
* “UFC This Week”

That gives the feed editorial structure.

## 2. LIVE PAGE — lean harder into speed and obsession

This should be the page people live in.

Right now it’s clean, but it can hit harder.

### Redesign:

* sticky date rail at top
* persistent “Live now” summary bar
* smart grouping by league with collapse/expand
* each game row should have:

  * score
  * period/status
  * possession / bases occupied / on-ice PP / red zone when available

Make the **game row itself more informative without making it busy**.

Sofascore wins because users feel informed instantly.
You need that same snap, but prettier.

## 3. NEWS PAGE — this should be one of your signature weapons

Your AI article modes are genuinely interesting: Pro, Simple, Toddler, and Translate . So the News page should feel built around that, not like a normal feed.

### Fix the feed:

Instead of plain news cards, introduce article identity:

* topic chip
* why it matters
* reading time
* freshness
* team/league relevance
* whether AI modes are available instantly or cached

### Better layout:

Have three feed styles users can toggle:

* Headlines
* Stories
* Deep Dive

### On article open:

This is where you separate from ESPN.

At top:

* headline
* source
* image
* relevance tag
* AI mode pills

Then the article body switches in place when modes change.
No floating bubble. No separate summary card. The text itself changes. That matches your own stated intent .

Also:

* long press preview on mobile
* hover preview on web
* “why this matters” inline snippet before the body
* “related games/teams/players” under article

That makes the article page feel connected to the rest of the OS.

# Big design system upgrades I’d make

## 1. Better hierarchy

You need more contrast between:

* page titles
* section headers
* metadata
* score numbers
* sublabels
* tertiary info

Right now too much of the UI lives in the same visual weight class.

## 2. More purposeful spacing

Some cards feel packed while some sections feel floaty.
You need a tighter spacing system:

* 4
* 8
* 12
* 16
* 24
* 32

And stick to it everywhere.

## 3. More visual identity beyond orange

Orange is cool, but if every “special” thing is orange, nothing is special.

Use accent logic:

* orange = interaction / brand
* red = live / urgent
* green = rise / win / clinch
* blue = info / neutral active
* purple = premium AI / special mode

That lets the product breathe.

## 4. Stronger card variety

You need 4–5 card families, not one family used everywhere.

For example:

* hero cards
* game rows
* story cards
* standings rows
* stat modules
* insight chips

## 5. Motion language

Add subtle motion rules:

* live pulse every 2.5s
* card press depth
* score-change flash
* chip selection glide
* article mode text dissolve
* standings row expand animation

That makes it feel modern.

# Product features that would make it better than ESPN and Sofascore

Not just prettier. Actually better.

## 1. Context engine

Every item should answer:

* why am I seeing this?
* why does this matter?
* what changed?

Example:
“Raptors lead by 30 — biggest 4Q margin swing tonight.”

That’s sticky.

## 2. My narrative feed

Not just scores.
Not just news.
Storylines.

Examples:

* “Rockets have won 4 straight”
* “Knicks drop into play-in danger”
* “Astros bullpen ERA worst in AL over last 7 days”

That’s where you become a sports OS.

## 3. Watch mode

A dedicated mode where users follow:

* favorite games
* key plays
* stat milestones
* upset alerts
* fantasy relevant updates
* betting relevant shifts

## 4. Instant explainer layer

A tap on any big headline or standings shift should answer:

* what happened
* why it matters
* what’s next

## 5. Smarter team pages

Your team and player pages already have the base pieces like roster, records, recent games, and player game logs . Build on that with:

* form trends
* injury impact
* lineup continuity
* recent matchup results
* fan sentiment pulse
* player role tags

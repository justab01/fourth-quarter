Rewrite this sports story so a fan of [target sport] can immediately understand its significance. Use the language, concepts, pacing, and cultural logic of [target sport] to explain what happened. Why this is better

Your current prompt risks becoming too gimmicky or too literal.

Important guardrails for output quality

This is probably part of why it “doesn’t sound right.”

You need these rules in the system prompt for all AI modes:
	•	preserve core facts
	•	do not invent stats or context
	•	do not exaggerate
	•	do not turn the story into a joke
	•	keep names, teams, and outcomes accurate
	•	do not overwrite uncertainty with fake certainty
	•	respect injuries, trades, suspensions, legal matters, and sensitive topics with a serious tone

That last one matters a lot.
You do not want playful output on serious stories.

⸻

Add tone control by story type

This is a hidden quality upgrade.

For certain article categories, the rewrite should stay more serious:
	•	injuries
	•	arrests
	•	deaths
	•	abuse allegations
	•	suspensions
	•	legal disputes
	•	firings
	•	medical news

For these, even Easy or Quick should stay respectful and straight.

So your backend should detect article type or keywords and apply a stricter rewrite tone.

Best final version to hand to your dev/build system

Here’s the clean product rewrite:

Reading Modes

Fourth Quarter lets users switch between multiple ways of reading the same sports story, depending on how much detail or context they want.

Original

Shows the article exactly as published by the source. No AI rewrite is used.

Easy

Rewrites the story in clear, natural language for casual fans, removing unnecessary jargon while preserving the key facts.

Quick

Condenses the story into a short, highly readable summary focused on the main takeaway.

Cross-Sport

Reframes the story using the language and logic of another sport, helping fans from different sports backgrounds understand why the story matters.

All AI-generated modes are cached by article and mode. Cross-Sport is additionally cached by target sport. Switching back to a previously loaded mode should feel instant. The article body itself should change in place — not through a pop-up or separate summary box.

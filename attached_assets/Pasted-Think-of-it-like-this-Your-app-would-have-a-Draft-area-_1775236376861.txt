Think of it like this:

Your app would have a Draft area the same way ESPN has scores, standings, and stats.

A normal fan would use it in a few simple ways.

1. “I wanna see who my team can draft”

They open:
	•	NFL
	•	tap Draft
	•	see:
	•	draft order
	•	their team’s picks
	•	top prospects
	•	latest mock drafts

So if I’m a Texans fan, I could open Texans or NFL Draft and instantly see:
	•	what pick Houston has
	•	who people think they’ll take
	•	what positions they need
	•	if they traded any picks away

That’s probably the most common use.

⸻

2. “I wanna see the best prospects”

They open:
	•	NBA Draft
	•	tap Prospects

Then they see a clean list like:
	•	Cooper Flagg
	•	Dylan Harper
	•	Ace Bailey

And each prospect card would show stuff like:
	•	position
	•	school/team
	•	height/weight
	•	projected draft range
	•	quick scouting summary

So it would feel kinda like player pages, but for players who aren’t in the league yet.

⸻

3. “I wanna follow the draft live”

On draft night, the Draft page would switch into live mode.

So instead of just static info, it would show:
	•	Pick 1 — team on the clock
	•	who got picked
	•	best available prospects left
	•	trade alerts
	•	quick grade or fit summary

So a person could sit there during the draft and follow every pick in real time.

Basically like a score page, but for the draft.

⸻

4. “I wanna know what happened in past drafts”

They open:
	•	History

Then they can look at:
	•	2025 draft
	•	2024 draft
	•	2023 draft

And tap a team or player to see:
	•	who was picked
	•	what round
	•	what number
	•	which team drafted them

That makes the app feel way deeper and more official.

What it would look like

League page example

Let’s say somebody opens NBA.

At the top tabs they might see:
	•	Scores
	•	Teams
	•	Standings
	•	Players
	•	Draft
	•	News

Then inside Draft:
	•	Overview
	•	Order
	•	Prospects
	•	Mocks
	•	Tracker
	•	History

So it’s clean.
Not confusing.
Everything draft-related lives in one place.

⸻

Team page example

If they open the Rockets page, they’d see a section like:

Draft Assets
	•	2026 1st-round pick
	•	2026 2nd-round pick
	•	team needs: center, wing defense
	•	projected selection: Player X
	•	recent picks: last 5 years

So now the fan doesn’t have to leave the team page to understand the future.

⸻

Prospect page example

If they tap a prospect, the page could look like this:

Player header
	•	headshot
	•	name
	•	school/team
	•	position

Tabs
	•	Overview
	•	Stats
	•	Scouting
	•	Measurables
	•	Projection
	•	News

And on that page they’d see:
	•	height / weight
	•	strengths
	•	weaknesses
	•	projected round
	•	mock draft history
	•	team fits

So it feels just like a regular player page, but built for draft guys.

Best simple visual flow

Example: NFL fan experience

User opens app
→ taps NFL
→ taps Draft
→ sees:

Top section
	•	next draft countdown
	•	top prospects
	•	latest mock

Middle section
	•	draft order

Bottom section
	•	latest draft news
	•	history

That’s it.
Simple.
Useful.
Easy to understand.

⸻

For sports without drafts

This is where it stays smart.

If a sport doesn’t really use drafts, the user would not see “Draft.”

They’d see something that fits better, like:
	•	Transfers
	•	Prospects
	•	Recruiting
	•	Pipeline

So the app wouldn’t force the wrong system onto the wrong sport.

The easiest way to think about it

Your app would be showing fans:
	•	who’s coming next
	•	who their team can get
	•	what picks they have
	•	what happened live
	•	what happened in past drafts

So Draft becomes the future-building section of the app.

Scores = what happened
Stats = how they performed
Draft = who’s next

That’s the simple vision.

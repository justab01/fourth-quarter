Yeah — for these sports, you need to stop thinking “ESPN or nothing” and build a **global sport-by-sport source map**.

**Track & field is absolutely pullable.** World Athletics already publishes a global competition calendar/results database plus a stats zone with results, rankings/qualification-style data, records, and athlete/stat surfaces. So even though ESPN doesn’t give you a usable track endpoint, the sport itself does have official data surfaces you can build around. ([worldathletics.org][1])

**Moto is also very workable.** MotoGP has official results/standings pages and an official app/product layer built around results, standings, and live timing coverage. MXGP/FIM similarly exposes official standings, results, categories, calendar, and rankings across the world championship ecosystem. ([The Official Home of MotoGP][2])

**Deep boxing is doable too, but it has to be federated.** There is no single official boxing authority for everything, so your “truth layer” needs to combine sanctioning-body rankings and title status from WBA, WBC, IBF, and WBO. Those orgs all publish current ratings/champions/rankings on their official sites. ([World Boxing Association][3])

## How to build it so it doesn’t feel stitched together

Do **not** build one “other sports” scraper.

Build this:

```txt
Global Sports Orchestrator
├── TrackFieldProvider
├── MotoGPProvider
├── MotocrossProvider
├── BoxingProvider
├── MMAProvider
├── RugbyProvider
└── Normalizer Layer
```

Then normalize everything into shared internal objects:

* `Competition`
* `Event`
* `Match` or `Race` or `MeetResult`
* `Athlete`
* `Team`
* `Standings`
* `Rankings`
* `Championship`
* `Division`
* `Session`
* `Round`

That lets your frontend treat a boxing division, a MotoGP weekend, and a track meet as different sport views built on one system instead of random JSON blobs.
